What is the operating cycle?
the operating cycle is the days required for a business to receive inventory, sell the inventory and get the cash from the sale of the inventory, the operating cycle plays a major role in determining the efficiency of a business.
How to calculate operating cycle?
To calculate operating cycle you should use the following formula:
operating cycle = days inventory outstanding + days sales outstanding
where:
days inventory outstanding (DIO) = (average inventory / cost of goods sold) * 365 days
days sales outstanding (DSO) = (average accounts receivable / revenue) * 365 days
example: company x in it's first year had
revenue = $12,000,000
cost of goods sold (COGS) = $7,000,000
accounts receivable = $1,800,000
inventory = $2,100,000

Why use operating cycle?
the operating cycle is used because it offers an insight into a company's operating efficiency. A shorter cycle indicates a more efficient and successful business because a short cycle is in an indication that a company is able to get enough cash to meet obligations and if the companys operating cycle is long then this company might have cash flow problems.
